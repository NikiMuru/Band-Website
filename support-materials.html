Standards

Band Member Expectations

Membership in the Washington Huskies Band is a privilege that carries real responsibilities. Every member agrees to uphold these standards when they join.

🎯 Preparation & Performance

✓
Know your music before rehearsalMembers are expected to practice their individual parts at home. Group rehearsal is for ensemble work, not learning notes.
✓
Arrive 10 minutes earlyCall time means ready to play — not walking in the door. Set up your instrument, warm up individually, and be in formation before the director takes the podium.
✓
Memorize all music by specified datesMarching season requires all music memorized before the first competition. Concert season has page deadlines communicated in the Band App.
✓
Maintain your instrumentDaily swabbing, weekly key oiling, and quarterly cork inspections. Equipment failures due to neglect are the student's responsibility to address before next rehearsal.
✓
Wear full, correct uniformAny missing uniform component at a performance is a deduction. No jewelry, unsanctioned footwear, or alterations to the issued uniform.

🤝 Conduct & Community

✓
Treat all members and staff with respectSection leaders have authority in the director's absence. Disagreements are resolved privately — never during rehearsal, on the field, or in uniform at events.
✓
No phones during rehearsalPhones are stored in phone pockets during rehearsals. Any call or text during warm-up is a privilege violation. If you don't own a phone, talk to Dr Edwards about what to do next.
✓
Mandatory attendance policyUnexcused absences the week before competitions result in a being unable to compete. You may be excused from a maximum of two absences from competitions before incurring additional work to make up for your absence.
✓
Zero tolerance for substance useAny student found in possession of or under the influence of substances at any band event is subject to immediate removal and school code of conduct proceedings.
✓
Represent the program honorablyIn uniform, you are representing the entire Washington High School band. Behavior at competitions, travel, and community events reflects on the entire band. Act accordingly.
Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions parents and new students ask most. Don't see yours? Email Dr. Edwards directly.

What are the program fees for the 2025–26 school year?
▾
The annual band participation fee is $250 per student, due in full or in two seperate installments by September 1st. This covers competition entry fees, transportation, and uniform costs. It does not cover the cost of marching shoes or personal instrument supplies. Financial hardship accommodations are available — contact Dr.Edwards confidentially.
Does the school provide instruments, or does my student need to bring their own?
▾
Washington High School maintains a full instrument lending library for students who cannot provide their own. School instruments are available on a first-come, first-served basis each August. A $50 annual maintenance deposit is required for borrowed instruments, fully refundable upon return in good condition. Students who own their own instrument are encouraged to use it, though school instruments may be used for marching to protect personal property.
How do I know about schedule changes and last-minute event updates?
▾
All schedule changes, weather cancellations, and event-day updates are communicated via StudentSquare. Students and parents can also download the Remind app with notifications enabled for communication on competition day. Download it from the App Store or Google Play.
Can my student join band mid-year, or is it September only?
▾
Mid-year enrollment is possible for the Concert Band and Jazz Ensemble, but marching band requires a full-season commitment starting in August pre-season. Students transferring in from other schools with marching experience may audition for the marching ensemble at the director's discretion. Contact Dr. Edwards to discuss your student's background and options.
What happens if my student misses a competition?
▾
Competition absences are taken very seriously, as they affect the entire ensemble's performance and score. If a student must miss a competition due to illness or a family emergency, the director must be notified at least 5 days in advance where possible. Three competition absences in a season will result in removal from the competitive ensemble for that year. There is no fee refund for missed competitions.
Are parents allowed to watch rehearsals?
▾
Indoor rehearsals in the band room are generally closed to parents to minimize distraction. Outdoor full-ensemble rehearsals (held on the practice field, usually Saturday mornings) are open for parents to observe from the designated spectator areas. The last Saturday rehearsal before each competition is typically an invited "dress run" — details are announced via Band App.
How do I volunteer to be a chaperone for away events?
▾
All chaperones must complete a DCPS background check before participating. Once cleared, sign up through the Volunteer page or contact our volunteer coordinator at boosters@washingtonhigh.edu. Chaperone slots are limited and filled in the order applications are received. Priority is given to parents of current band members who have completed orientation.
My student is interested in drum major. How does that process work?
▾
Drum major auditions are held each May for the following fall season. Candidates must have at least one full year of marching experience, demonstrate strong musical knowledge across multiple sections, and pass a conducting and marching clinic evaluated by the director and staff. A leadership interview is also required. Announcements are made in April via Band App with audition packet details.
