Travel Safety

Chaperone Guidelines

All chaperones must read and acknowledge these guidelines before participating in any away event. Questions can be directed to Dr. Edwards.

📋 General Conduct Rules

1
Background Check Required: All chaperones must complete a Washington DC Public Schools background screening before the first event. Processing takes 5–10 business days.
2
Student Supervision Ratio: Maintain no less than a 1:8 adult-to-student ratio at all times during travel, hotel stays, and non-performance periods.
3
No Personal Devices During Show: Chaperones should not be on personal phones while actively supervising students in warm-up, performance, or transition areas.
4
Alcohol & Substance Policy: Zero tolerance. No consumption of alcohol or substances during any band-related event from departure to return.
5
Medical Emergencies: All chaperones must review student medical alert files distributed by the director before each event. Carry the emergency contact binder.
6
Communication: Chaperones must have the Band App installed and notifications enabled. The primary director will use the app's broadcast feature for updates.

✈️ Overnight Travel Policy

A
Room Assignments: Students are assigned roommates by the director. Chaperone rooms are adjacent to student floors. Room checks occur at 10:30 PM and lights out at 11:00 PM.
B
Cross-Gender Room Policy: Students may not enter rooms assigned to students of a different gender under any circumstances. Violations result in immediate parent notification and potential dismissal from the trip.
C
Meal Accountability: All students must eat as a group during designated meal windows. No student may leave the designated dining area without chaperone escort.
D
Free Time Supervision: Any free time at the event venue must be supervised. Students are not to leave venue grounds. A check-in buddy system is enforced at all times.
E
Social Media: Chaperones should not post identifiable photos of minors to social media without Booster Club pre-approval. Direct all media to the official media volunteer.
F
Incident Reporting: Any behavioral or safety incident must be documented on a Booster Club incident form and submitted to the director within 24 hours of the event.
Competition Day

Performance Day Itinerary

A standard timeline for a competitive marching band event. Exact times vary per competition and will be communicated via the Band App.

🌅
6:00 AM — Call Time
All-Hands Arrival at WHS Band Room
Students arrive in comfortable clothing. Directors take attendance. Equipment crew begins loading instrument and pit trucks. Instrument cases pre-staged the evening before.
🚌
7:00 AM — Departure
Buses Depart WHS Parking Lot
Charter buses depart in convoy. Uniform bags loaded in cargo hold. Students are briefed on competition rules, performance order, and arrival procedures by section leaders.
🅿️
Arrival — Venue Check-In
Director Check-In & Lot Assignment
Director checks in at the competition table. Equipment truck is directed to the unloading zone. Students stay on buses until a holding area is assigned.
🎽
T-minus 3 hrs — Uniform Up
Students Change into Full Uniform
Uniform crew assists with shako placement, gauntlet fastening, and bib adjustment. Director inspects each section. Full uniform is worn from this point through awards.
🎼
T-minus 90 min — Warm-Up Block
Warm-Up Area: Full Ensemble Preparation
45-minute warm-up in the designated area. Brass warm-up, battery/front ensemble tuning, and full run-through at 75% visual energy. Chaperones secure the perimeter.
🚶
T-minus 30 min — Gate Walk
March from Warm-Up to Gate Staging
The ensemble marches in full formation from the warm-up lot to the field entrance gate. Drum majors lead. Silence protocol observed from this point through exit.
🏆
Performance — Show Time
Competitive Field Performance
9–15 minute competitive program. Chaperones and parents watch from the designated section. No one enters the press box area. Photography from parent section only.
🍽️
Post-Show — Meal Break
Team Meal & Rest Period
Students change out of uniforms. Meal crew distributes team dinner. Students may watch other bands perform during this time under chaperone supervision.
🎖️
Awards Ceremony
Full Ensemble Gathers for Awards
All students attend awards in uniform. Drum majors accept trophies. Group photo taken immediately following. Social media encouraged — tag @whsbandofficial.
🏠
Return — Departure for WHS
Load Up & Return Trip
Equipment loaded, buses depart. ETA communicated via Band App. Parents should expect pickup at WHS 30 minutes after bus departure from venue. Students unload equipment upon return.
